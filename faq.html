FAQ

What do we do?

Emoti is educational technology aimed at grades 5-8 that uses virtual reality to teach emotional self-recognition and regulation in order to create a personalized Social Emotional Learning (SEL) educational experience for all. Psychology studies have shown time and again the positive impact of emotional intelligence on personal, professional, and academic futures.

How does Emoti work?

We teach social and emotional skills by giving students VR simulations and measuring emotional response to them. We measure physiological signs like heart rate and skin conductance, mapping them to emotional states. This lets us quantitatively track students' emotions within the simulation, and can change the simulation as necessary to elicit the appropriate emotional response.

Why VR?

Educational videos about emotions just don't have the same emotional impact as Virtual Reality (VR). VR allows people to fully immerse themselves into strange worlds they've never seen before– or worlds that are all-too-familiar to them. VR allows students to react to a virtual world in the same way they would to the real world, instead of giving canned, "correct" answers to a teacher on paper.

Why can't teachers teach emotional skills?

They can. But teachers often don't have the bandwidth or ability to teach entire classrooms social and emotional skills. Often teaching these skills requires special training. Even trained teachers at the schools we've visited have expressed their interest in Emoti's ability to standardize– yet still personalize– an emotional learning experience for every student.
